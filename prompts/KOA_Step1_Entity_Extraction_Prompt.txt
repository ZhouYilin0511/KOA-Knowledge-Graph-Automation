For bone mineral density measurement, the finding should be extracted from the textual conclusion part, such as “骨量减少”, while specific numerical value descriptions should be ignored. When a finding contains pure numerical values in parentheses (such as measurement values or T-scores) without arrow symbols such as ↑ or ↓, only the core descriptive part before the parentheses should be extracted and the numerical values ignored. However, grading or severity descriptions (such as “（2级）”) must be retained in full as part of the entity. When a Laboratory and imaging findings description contains delimiters such as commas, periods, enumeration commas, numbered lists (such as 1., 2., etc.), or the conjunctions “及”, “和”, or “或”, it must be split into multiple independent Laboratory and imaging findings entities. During splitting, the conjunctions or delimiters should be replaced with a comma “，” and complete phrases should be formed separately with the preceding and following components. For example, “股骨远端骨髓水肿及胫骨平台下方骨髓水肿” should be split into two independent entities: “股骨远端骨髓水肿” and “胫骨平台下方骨髓水肿”; “髌上囊积液、膝关节腔积液” should be split into “髌上囊积液” and “膝关节腔积液”. However, “膝关节髌上囊积液伴滑膜炎” must be extracted as one complete Laboratory and imaging findings entity and must not be split. Special attention: a Laboratory and imaging findings entity may be extracted only when its corresponding Auxiliary examination entity has also been extracted. For examinations that were planned but not actually performed, any expected or hypothetical results that may be mentioned must not be extracted. Laboratory and imaging findings entities must be extracted strictly and only from the “检查结果” field of the examination report. Any content from the “检查所见” field must never be extracted.
⑦ TCM syndrome: Refers to the summary of the pathogenesis and clinical manifestations of a disease at a specific stage in TCM syndrome differentiation. A single syndrome pattern should follow the format “xxxx证”; a compound syndrome pattern should follow the format “xxxx，xxxx证”, for example, “肾虚髓亏，气滞血瘀证”. TCM syndrome entities must be normalized and extracted strictly according to the above formats. For compound syndrome patterns, the entire expression must be extracted as one complete entity and must not be split into multiple independent TCM syndrome entities. In addition, the extracted TCM syndrome must be directly related to knee osteoarthritis. TCM syndrome diagnoses used for other comorbid diseases (such as “胸痹心痛”) must not be extracted. Special attention: 1. File-type restriction: TCM syndrome entities may be extracted only from the following three types of documents: admission records, first-course records (specifically the first course record written after admission, excluding the first postoperative course record or the first record after any other intervention), and discharge records. 2. Paragraph restriction: Only TCM syndrome entities explicitly documented in the diagnostic sections of the above three document types may be extracted. The “diagnostic section” includes any paragraph or sentence that explicitly records a TCM syndrome differentiation conclusion, such as those containing expressions with keywords like “辩证为”, “证属”, “中医证候”, or “诊断”. All text must be carefully scanned to ensure that no eligible TCM syndrome entity is omitted. Extraction from any other types of course records—such as first postoperative course records, ward-round notes, and daily progress notes—as well as from any non-diagnostic sections, is strictly prohibited. Before extraction, the paragraph type must first be confirmed (for example, based on the heading or context). Extraction may be performed only when the paragraph belongs to an allowed type. If the record type is a ward-round note, daily progress note, or any other non-permitted document type, extraction of TCM syndrome entities is absolutely prohibited regardless of the content.
⑧ TCM therapeutic method: Refers to the guiding principles of TCM treatment. The core criterion for identification is direct association with a TCM prescription, following the principle of “therapeutic method–prescription mapping”. This includes three situations: 1. There is an explicit expression related to a therapeutic method and it is associated with a TCM prescription, such as typical formulations including “治法”, “中医治法：…”, “以…为治法”, or “治法以…为原则”, together with a corresponding TCM prescription. 2. The term “治法” does not explicitly appear, but the context clearly indicates that the therapeutic method is directly bound to a TCM prescription, such as expressions like “予…[TCM prescription]…[therapeutic method 1][therapeutic method 2]”. In such cases, all parallel therapeutic-method expressions should be treated as TCM therapeutic method entities. 3. There is an explicit therapeutic-method expression that is directly associated with a TCM prescription used in conjunction with an Other TCM characteristic therapy, such as “予中医特色疗法…以[therapeutic method 1][therapeutic method 2]，方用[TCM prescription]”, where the therapeutic method serves as the guiding principle for the accompanying TCM prescription used with that characteristic therapy. In such cases, the therapeutic-method expression should be treated as a TCM therapeutic method entity. A TCM therapeutic method does not refer to a specific treatment modality. Specific interventions such as acupuncture, Tuina, and TCM directional drug penetration therapy are treatment modalities, and descriptions of their efficacy are not equivalent to TCM therapeutic method. A TCM therapeutic method must be directly associated with a TCM prescription for knee osteoarthritis. It should be extracted only when the therapeutic method serves as the guiding principle for that TCM prescription, and when the associated TCM prescription has been explicitly administered and extracted. The Context Anchor of a TCM therapeutic method must include a direct associative description with the corresponding TCM prescription name, in order to reflect the relationship of therapeutic method–prescription mapping. Special attention: extraction of a TCM therapeutic method entity strictly depends on extraction of the corresponding TCM prescription entity. Before extracting a TCM therapeutic method, it is mandatory to first check whether the associated TCM prescription entity has been excluded under any rule (such as disease-specific relevance or treatment-entity restrictions). If the corresponding TCM prescription entity has not been extracted for any reason, then the associated TCM therapeutic method entity must not be extracted. In addition, when extracting a TCM therapeutic method, the guiding principle of treatment must be strictly distinguished from descriptions of treatment efficacy. A TCM therapeutic method must be a guiding principle for treatment operations, whereas descriptions that directly modify a treatment operation should be treated as efficacy attributes and must not be extracted as TCM therapeutic method. If the description only refers to the efficacy of other therapies such as acupuncture or Tuina (for example, “针灸以…益气生血、疏经通络”), that efficacy description should be used only as Context Anchor content for the corresponding therapy and must not be extracted as a TCM therapeutic method entity. Formatting rules for TCM therapeutic method entities: When multiple therapeutic methods appear in the original text connected by commas, enumeration commas, or semantically parallel structures (such as “A、B” or “AB” structures), they must be split into multiple independent TCM therapeutic method entities. No connector or conjunction should be retained in the final entity string. For example, if the original text states “治以补益肝肾，通络止痛”, it must be extracted as two independent entities: “TCM therapeutic method: 补益肝肾” and “TCM therapeutic method: 通络止痛”. Similarly, if the original text states “予以独活寄生汤加减补益肝肾，活血通经”, where “补益肝肾” and “活血通经” are semantically parallel therapeutic-method expressions, they must be extracted as two independent entities: “TCM therapeutic method: 补益肝肾” and “TCM therapeutic method: 活血通经”. If the therapeutic-method description is a continuous string without any obvious connector or separator (such as “温经散寒止痛”), it must be extracted as one complete TCM therapeutic method entity and must not be split. Special attention: a TCM therapeutic method entity must be directly related to the treatment of knee osteoarthritis. Any TCM therapeutic method used for treating other comorbid diseases (such as “胸痹心痛”) must not be extracted. If the therapeutic-method description is intended for systemic regulation or for purposes not directly related to the joint (such as “补中益气”), it must not be extracted.
⑨ Chinese patent medicine: Refers to medicinal preparations manufactured according to fixed prescriptions, such as “仙灵骨葆胶囊”, “痹祺胶囊”, and “跌打消炎散”, as well as external TCM ointment preparations that do not contain chemically synthesized drug ingredients (such as “消炎止痛膏” and “麝香活血化瘀膏”). Chinese patent medicine includes all fixed-prescription preparations made from Chinese medicinal ingredients, regardless of dosage form (such as capsules, tablets, ointments, powders, aerosols, etc.). If the drug name contains typical Chinese medicinal components or common names of Chinese patent medicine (such as “麝香”, “红花”, “活血化瘀”, “跌打”, or “舒经活络”), it should be preferentially extracted as Chinese patent medicine and must not be omitted. Special attention: 1. Only Chinese patent medicine explicitly used for the treatment of knee osteoarthritis should be extracted. Chinese patent medicine used for treating other comorbid diseases must not be extracted. 2. Chinese patent medicine used only for the management of surgical complications in preoperative or postoperative treatment (such as thrombosis, infection, or wound healing) must be strictly excluded, even if the drug name itself is relevant. 3. If a Chinese patent medicine is used postoperatively to relieve the primary symptoms of knee osteoarthritis (such as pain or impaired joint mobility), and there is no wording indicating that it is intended only for complications, it must be extracted.
⑩ Chinese herbal medicine: Refers to natural medicinal plants, minerals, animals, and their processed products used in TCM as single herbs, such as “独活”, “牛膝”, and “威灵仙”. Descriptive modifiers of the herb form, such as “超微饮片”, do not need to be extracted; only the herb name itself should be extracted. Names of Chinese herbal medicine must be normalized (for example, “寄生” should be normalized to “桑寄生”, and “熟地” to “熟地黄”), but names related to processing or preparation methods of the medicinal material must not be altered. However, if a TCM prescription is unrelated to the treatment of knee osteoarthritis, its constituent Chinese herbal medicine entities must not be extracted. When a TCM prescription is described as “原方加减” or “续用原方”, and the current text does not explicitly provide the original formula name or the associated disease, relevance must be determined by evaluating the overall core therapeutic effect of the listed herbal composition: 1. Priority should be given to the classical efficacy of the original formula. For example, when “原方加减” appears, the original formula name may be inferred from the herbal composition, and its classical indications should then be used to judge whether it is related to knee osteoarthritis. If the inferred formula is a classical prescription such as “炙甘草汤”, and its classical therapeutic effect is not directed toward knee osteoarthritis, it should be directly judged as irrelevant. 2. The constituent Chinese herbal medicine should be extracted only when the overall core therapeutic effect is directly and primarily aimed at knee osteoarthritis, such as dispelling wind and eliminating dampness, dispersing cold and relieving pain, promoting blood circulation and removing blood stasis, unblocking the collaterals and relieving pain, tonifying the liver and kidney, or strengthening tendons and bones, and not primarily intended for treating diseases of other systems (such as cardiovascular, digestive, or respiratory diseases). If the overall core therapeutic effect is mainly directed toward other systemic diseases or toward general systemic regulation not directly related to the joints, then even if the prescription contains individual herbs associated with joint treatment (such as “伸筋草”), the entire TCM prescription and all of its constituent Chinese herbal medicine entities must not be extracted. Additional clarification: a Chinese herbal medicine entity should be extracted as part of a prescription composition only when the herb name explicitly appears in a TCM prescription ingredient list that includes a specific dosage (such as “柴胡10g”), or in a list explicitly introduced by markers such as “中药处方如下：” or “药用：”, where the list contains both specific herb names and dosages. Herb names mentioned only for efficacy analysis or pathogenesis explanation (for example, “方中苍术、黄柏清热利湿”), without specific dosages and not included in a prescription composition list, do not count as actual prescription constituents and must not be extracted.
⑪ TCM prescription: Refers to compound herbal prescriptions decocted according to clinical compatibility principles, including formula names and their modifiers such as “加减”, as well as herbal compound prescriptions used in conjunction with Other TCM characteristic therapy that contain specific Chinese herbal medicine ingredients. The core requirement is to identify any prescription whose name explicitly contains “中药” or otherwise clearly implies herbal ingredients. As long as the context indicates that it is used for the treatment of knee osteoarthritis, it should be extracted. Priority must be given to directly extracting the explicit core TCM prescription name exactly as stated in the original text. Descriptive phrases or secondary contextual expressions must not be used as the prescription name. Only when the original text does not explicitly provide any prescription name at all may a name be flexibly inferred from the context. Irrelevant modifiers such as anatomical prefixes (e.g., “膝关节”), prefixes describing the application target or scenario (e.g., “痛点”, “穴位”, “局部”), and suffixes such as “治疗” must be removed, and only the core prescription name should be retained. However, note that the core TCM prescription name may itself include meaningful prefixes (such as “特大” in “特大中药封包”) or suffixes (such as “加减” in “独活寄生汤加减”), which must be retained. Examples include “独活寄生汤加减”, “独活寄生汤”, “中药热奄包”, “中药封包”, and “特大中药封包”. A key characteristic of this entity type is that it usually includes specific Chinese herbal medicine ingredients. However, one special case must be emphasized: even if no specific Chinese herbal medicine list is provided, any entity whose prescription name explicitly contains the term “中药” (such as “中药热奄包” or “中药封包”, or other typical herbal-prescription-style names), and which is explicitly mentioned in a treatment context and directly used for the treatment of knee osteoarthritis (with no wording indicating that it is intended for other comorbid diseases or surgical complications), must still be extracted as a TCM prescription entity. In such cases, extraction must not depend on the presence of a Chinese herbal medicine list, and omission due to the absence of herb names is strictly prohibited. It must be especially emphasized that when the original text has already explicitly provided a TCM prescription name (such as “特大中药封包”, “独活寄生汤加减”, “四妙散加减”, “自拟方”, or “中药硬膏热贴敷”), that explicit core TCM prescription name must be used strictly and with absolute priority. It must not be replaced or influenced by any subsequently appearing descriptions of dosage form, administration, or other contextual information. No alternative name may be inferred merely because the context contains a Chinese herbal medicine composition list or efficacy description. During extraction, the word “治疗” must be removed, and only the core prescription name should be retained. No arbitrary reformulation or modification is allowed. Adding “方” or any other suffix, prefix, or extra wording is strictly prohibited. The literal form of the original text must be preserved completely, and no additional characters may be introduced. However, one special exception must be observed: for the TCM prescription used in conjunction with the Other TCM characteristic therapy entity “中医定向透药”, the prescription name must be uniformly normalized as “中医定向透药疗法方”, regardless of whether the original text provides any other name. This is a mandatory normalization requirement. Only when, and only when, the original text does not explicitly provide any TCM prescription name at all (i.e., there is no explicit prescription naming such as “XX方” or “XX汤”), but the text simultaneously provides both a specific Chinese herbal medicine composition and a corresponding TCM therapeutic method, may a prescription name be flexibly inferred from the context. For example, if the herbal compound prescription is used for 中医定向透药疗法, it may be named “中医定向透药疗法方”; if it is used for another therapy or context, a name may be inferred on the basis of the therapeutic method or therapy name, such as “补肾益气活血方”. Any inferred name must be closely grounded in the context and must reasonably reflect the content. For example, if the original text states “中医定向透药疗法治疗膝关节以补肾益气、活血止痛，方中熟地、杜仲、牛膝、寄生以补肝肾、强筋骨...”, it may be extracted as “TCM prescription: 中医定向透药疗法方 :: [full original description]”. Similarly, if the original text states “治以活血化瘀、通络止痛，药用桃仁、红花、牛膝等”, the prescription name may be inferred as “活血化瘀通络方”. It is strictly prohibited to infer any TCM prescription name when only the therapy name (such as “中医定向透药” or “穴位贴敷”) and/or efficacy descriptions (such as “舒筋活络” or “止痛”) are mentioned, but no specific Chinese herbal medicine composition is described and no corresponding association between TCM therapeutic method and herbs is provided. In such cases, only the corresponding therapy entity should be extracted under its own category (such as Other TCM characteristic therapy or Acupuncture therapy), and no additional TCM prescription entity may be extracted. Note, however, that when a description of “穴位贴敷” includes a specific Chinese herbal medicine composition, it must also be extracted as a TCM prescription entity. In such cases, the prescription name should be inferred as “穴位贴敷方”, and the Context Anchor should include the complete prescription description, including the Chinese herbal medicine list. This does not affect extraction of “穴位贴敷” itself as an Acupuncture therapy entity (see the rules for category ⑮). Special attention: only TCM prescription entities explicitly used for the treatment of knee osteoarthritis should be extracted, including prescriptions used directly to treat the primary symptoms of knee osteoarthritis (such as pain) and directly related adjunctive treatment for knee osteoarthritis (such as analgesia or relief of swelling). However, the following categories strictly related to preoperative/postoperative treatment associated with surgery must be excluded from extraction: 1. Prescriptions used for preoperative preparation for knee osteoarthritis surgery (such as preoperative thrombosis prevention); 2. Prescriptions used for adjunctive treatment of specific complications arising during or after surgery (such as postoperative hemostasis, anticoagulation, thrombosis prevention, anti-infection treatment, or promotion of wound healing); 3. Prescriptions used to treat all pathological states induced after surgery (such as postoperative qi-blood conflict, impaired circulation, or blood stasis accumulation). TCM prescription entities used for other comorbid diseases (such as “胸痹心痛”), or prescriptions whose treatment site is explicitly a body part other than the knee joint, must not be extracted. This rule is fully aligned with the above Chinese herbal medicine rules. For prescriptions such as “原方加减” whose associated disease is not explicitly stated, the relevance must likewise be judged by inferring the classical efficacy of the original formula and the theme of its overall core therapeutic effect from the herbal composition: if the classical efficacy of the original formula is not directed toward knee osteoarthritis, or if the overall core therapeutic effect is primarily intended for treatment of diseases of other systems, then even if the prescription contains individual related herbs, the TCM prescription entity must still be judged as unrelated to knee osteoarthritis and must not be extracted. Additional clarification: the Context Anchor of a TCM prescription should include only the prescription composition list containing specific dosages and the directly associated TCM therapeutic method descriptions. Descriptions of Chinese herbal medicine names used only for efficacy analysis and lacking dosage information (such as “方中苍术、黄柏清热利湿”) must be excluded. Information unrelated to the actual prescription composition—such as acupuncture point selection or the names of other therapies—must also be excluded. The Context Anchor must reflect only the actual prescription composition and the associated therapeutic method, without redundant or interfering information.
⑫ Western medicine: Refers to chemically synthesized therapeutic drugs or biological agents, such as “塞来昔布”, “玻璃酸钠注射液”, and “洛索洛芬钠凝胶贴膏”. Western medicine does not include Chinese patent medicine. If a drug name is clearly a TCM preparation or contains Chinese medicinal ingredients, it should be classified as a Chinese patent medicine entity. When a Western medicine description contains the connector “+”, or when “+” is absent but the context clearly indicates a mixed infusion or sequential administration scenario of the form solvent + drug (such as common clinical expressions like “XX注射液XXml XX注射液续滴” or “XX注射液 XX注射液静滴”), the drugs are being used in combination and must be extracted as one complete Western medicine entity. In such cases, the entity name must retain the “+” connector, for example, “0.9%氯化钠注射液+地塞米松磷酸钠注射液”. When the same Western medicine name appears multiple times in the text, it should be merged into a single entity. The Context Anchor must include all complete sentences corresponding to all relevant occurrences, so as to cover all information related to that drug. It must not be split into multiple independent entities. The Context Anchor must consist of the minimum complete sentences quoted verbatim from the original text that collectively cover all relevant occurrences, thereby ensuring accurate matching of all related contexts and avoiding fragmentation. Special emphasis: when a Western medicine entity serves as the injected drug component of the Acupuncture therapy entity “穴位注射”, that Western medicine entity must also be extracted separately. For example, in “穴位注射维生素B1注射液”, two entities must be extracted separately: “Acupuncture therapy: 穴位注射” and “Western medicine: 维生素B1注射液”. The Western medicine entity must not be omitted simply because the Acupuncture therapy entity has been extracted. If the acupoint injection contains a Western medicine component, it must be extracted as a Western medicine entity, and its standard injection dosage-form suffix must be completed according to the route of administration (acupoint injection). For example, “维生素B12” should be completed as “维生素B12注射液”. The Context Anchor must clearly reflect the associated scenario of “穴位注射”, ensuring that the entity name is complete and not omitted. Special attention: only Western medicine explicitly used for the treatment of knee osteoarthritis should be extracted. This includes drugs used directly to treat symptoms of knee osteoarthritis (such as pain and swelling), as well as adjunctive treatment directly related to knee osteoarthritis (such as calcium supplementation or anti-osteoporosis treatment for osteoporosis). The following must be strictly excluded: adjunctive treatment aimed at specific complications arising during or after surgery (such as postoperative anticoagulation, anti-infection treatment, postoperative analgesia used only for surgical wounds or postoperative recovery, and wound swelling reduction), as well as Western medicine used to treat symptoms unrelated to knee osteoarthritis (such as insomnia, poor night sleep, anxiety, and other non-KOA-related symptoms). These must not be extracted. If a Western medicine drug is used for analgesic treatment of primary knee osteoarthritis symptoms (such as knee pain not caused by surgical trauma), for example “洛索洛芬钠凝胶贴膏”, then even if it is used postoperatively and the description contains wording such as “解热镇痛”, it still counts as direct treatment of knee osteoarthritis symptoms and must be extracted normally. It must not be classified under the excluded category of “surgery-related postoperative analgesia”. Even if the drug description does not explicitly mention the character “膝”, as long as the context indicates that it is used for treatment related to knee osteoarthritis or for relief of symptoms related to knee osteoarthritis, and it does not fall within the above exclusion criteria, it should be extracted normally. Drugs mentioned as discharge medications must not be extracted, because the discharge medication section is excluded from extraction. Western medicine used to treat other comorbid diseases (such as hypertension, coronary heart disease, or hyperlipidemia) must not be extracted. Additional clarification: if the original text mentions only the core drug ingredient name (such as “七叶皂苷钠”, “甲钴胺”, “玻璃酸钠”, or “维生素B12”) without explicitly stating the dosage form, but the route of administration (such as oral administration, injection, or acupoint injection) makes it possible to determine the omitted common dosage-form suffix (such as “片” for oral administration or “注射液” for injection), and the drug is a commonly used clinical preparation, then the dosage-form suffix must be reasonably completed according to the route of administration so as to form a complete Western medicine entity name. For example, “七叶皂苷钠” should be completed as “七叶皂苷钠片”, “甲钴胺” as “甲钴胺片”, and “玻璃酸钠” administered by injection as “玻璃酸钠注射液”, so as to ensure that the entity name is complete and standardized.
⑬ Western non-pharmacological therapy: Refers to physical therapy and rehabilitation interventions in Western medicine that do not rely on medication and are used for the treatment or rehabilitation of knee osteoarthritis. These include, but are not limited to, typical examples such as “踝泵训练”, “股四头肌收缩训练”, “助步器辅助下部分负重行走”, “康复锻炼”, and “超短波短波治疗”, as well as various specific training, exercise, and rehabilitation procedures. Even if such rehabilitation training or physical therapy is performed postoperatively, it should still be regarded as directly related to knee osteoarthritis and extracted, as long as its purpose is to improve knee joint function and promote recovery from osteoarthritis rather than merely to facilitate surgical wound healing or manage surgical complications. However, this category does not include general behavioral advice, preventive measures, or simple descriptions of equipment use, such as “减少负重”, “卧床休息”, or “防跌仆”. During extraction, irrelevant verbs without substantive meaning in the entity description (such as “行” or “予”, which serve only as introductory words) must be removed, and only the core therapy name should be extracted. If the entity description contains supplementary information in parentheses, the parentheses and their contents must be retained only when the parenthetical content provides useful information such as the anatomical site or acupoint location. If the content within parentheses refers only to irrelevant information such as time or frequency, both the parentheses and the time/frequency content within them must be removed, and only the core therapy name should be extracted. For example, if the original text states “予关节松动训练（膝关节）qd以改善关节活动”, it should be extracted as “关节松动训练（膝关节）”, where “（膝关节）” is retained because it indicates the anatomical site, whereas “qd” is removed because it indicates frequency. Special attention: only Western non-pharmacological therapy clearly shown by the context to be used for treating knee osteoarthritis or promoting its rehabilitation should be extracted. Western non-pharmacological therapy used for treating other comorbid diseases must not be extracted. At the same time, therapies used in surgery-related preoperative or postoperative rehabilitation that are aimed solely at surgical wounds or complications (such as postoperative wound care or drainage) must be strictly excluded and must not be extracted. Western non-pharmacological therapy mentioned in discharge instructions must not be extracted, because the discharge instruction section is excluded from extraction. However, if an entity appears in the form of “康复治疗”, “嘱”, or other directive expressions, it should still be extracted as long as it is related to the treatment of knee osteoarthritis and does not belong to the discharge instruction section.
⑭ Other TCM characteristic therapy: Refers to TCM disease-intervention methods that do not rely on Chinese herbal medicine or Acupuncture therapy. This category absolutely excludes needling methods and moxibustion methods, but includes manual techniques related to acupoints (such as “手指点穴” and “推拿”). Examples include “推拿”, “拔罐”, “刮痧”, “放血”, “关节粘连传统松解术”, “手指点穴”, and “中医定向透药”, as well as any other TCM physical or manual therapies. The core requirement is to identify any TCM therapeutic procedure that is neither Chinese herbal medicine nor Acupuncture therapy. As long as the original text explicitly mentions a specific therapy name and it is used for the treatment of knee osteoarthritis, it should be extracted and must not be omitted. When extracting Other TCM characteristic therapy, irrelevant verbs (such as “予”) must be strictly ignored, and only the core therapy name should be extracted. If the description contains parentheses, the parenthetical content should be retained only when it provides useful information such as the anatomical site or acupoint location. If the content within parentheses indicates only irrelevant modifiers such as time or frequency, the parenthetical content should be ignored and only the core therapy name should be extracted. Extraction of Other TCM characteristic therapy must remain strictly faithful to the original therapy name and must not be arbitrarily simplified or modified. For example, if the original text states “拔火罐”, it must be extracted as “拔火罐” and must not be simplified as “拔罐”. However, for expressions such as “其他推拿手法治疗” or “其他推拿治疗”, where the modifier “其他” is present but the core procedure is clearly “推拿”, the entity should be uniformly simplified and extracted as “推拿”. In addition, if the therapy name in the original text ends with the word “疗法”, the word “疗法” should be uniformly removed and only the core name should be extracted. For example, “中医定向透药疗法” should be normalized and extracted as “中医定向透药”, “拔罐疗法” as “拔罐”, and “放血疗法” as “放血”. The same principle applies to other similar cases. Special attention: Other TCM characteristic therapy may involve the use of medication, but the therapy itself still belongs to this entity type. If the therapy description contains a specific Chinese herbal medicine composition, then a TCM prescription entity should also be additionally extracted; however, this does not affect the extraction of the Other TCM characteristic therapy entity itself. For example, “中医定向透药” should be extracted as an Other TCM characteristic therapy entity, while the herbal prescription used with it should be extracted separately as a TCM prescription entity. However, if the therapy description itself contains specific herbal prescription forms such as “中药封包” or “中药热奄包”, then that entity should be classified as a TCM prescription rather than as Other TCM characteristic therapy. Only Other TCM characteristic therapy explicitly used for the treatment of knee osteoarthritis should be extracted. Therapies of this type used for treating other comorbid diseases must not be extracted. At the same time, Other TCM characteristic therapy used in surgery-related preoperative or postoperative treatment and aimed solely at complications must be strictly excluded. If such a therapy is used postoperatively to relieve the primary symptoms of knee osteoarthritis and there is no wording indicating that it is intended only for complications, then it must be extracted. In addition, if the description of an Other TCM characteristic therapy does not explicitly specify the treatment site (for example, it does not mention specific sites such as “腰部” or “上肢”), but it appears in the context of a knee osteoarthritis medical record and there is no other wording indicating that it is intended for a comorbid disease or a surgical complication, then it should be regarded as being directly used for the treatment of knee osteoarthritis and must be extracted.
⑮ Acupuncture therapy: Refers to TCM therapeutic methods that prevent or treat disease by stimulating specific sites on the human body (Acupoint), mainly including needling methods and moxibustion methods. Examples include “针刺” (if the original text contains any synonymous expression involving “针刺”, such as “普通针刺”, “常规针刺”, or “常规行针刺”, regardless of whether the prefix is “普通”, “常规”, or any similar modifier, it must be uniformly normalized and extracted as “针刺”; the original expression must not be retained), “电针”, “头皮针”, “梅花针”, “温针灸”, “隔物灸” (such as “隔姜灸”), “穴位贴敷” (special attention: if the original text incorrectly writes this as “穴位帖敷”, it must be corrected and uniformly extracted as the correct form “穴位贴敷”; the erroneous form must never be retained), “穴位注射”, “耳穴压豆”, and “耳针”. Composite therapy names must be identified strictly. When the therapy name is a clearly designated specific name (such as the comprehensive therapy “三通四联”), the complete original name, including quotation marks or any other punctuation in the original text, must be fully extracted without any abbreviation, omission, or modification. By contrast, for expressions such as “针灸综合治疗” that do not constitute a clearly designated specific name, only the core therapy name “针灸” should be extracted, and the words “综合治疗” do not need to be retained. Special attention: when multiple independent Acupuncture therapy modalities are mentioned within the same description (for example, “针刺、电针、腕踝针”), entities must be extracted separately for each therapy type. In addition, the Context Anchor for each therapy must be matched exclusively to that therapy, meaning that only the efficacy description and Acupoint list directly corresponding to that therapy should be retained, while all content related to other acupuncture therapies must be completely removed. This ensures a one-to-one correspondence between the context and the therapy, without redundant information. Special emphasis: “穴位注射” belongs to the category of Acupuncture therapy. When the original text contains compound descriptions such as “穴位注射维生素B1注射液”, both “Acupuncture therapy: 穴位注射” and the corresponding Western medicine entity “Western medicine: 维生素B1注射液” must be extracted. Neither may be omitted. The Acupuncture therapy entity for acupoint injection and the corresponding Western medicine entity must be extracted separately, and they do not conflict with each other. Special attention: “穴位贴敷” must always be classified as an Acupuncture therapy entity, regardless of whether it contains a specific Chinese herbal medicine composition. When “穴位贴敷” is treated as Acupuncture therapy, its corresponding Acupoint entities must be extracted normally (see the rules for category ⑰). If “穴位贴敷” includes a specific Chinese herbal medicine composition, it must also be extracted as a TCM prescription entity (see the rules for category ⑪), but extraction of the Acupuncture therapy entity remains independent. Its Context Anchor should cite the minimum complete phrase containing the therapy operation and the Acupoint list, excluding the Chinese herbal medicine description. Acupuncture therapy does not include manual therapies such as “手指点穴” or “推拿”; these should be classified under Other TCM characteristic therapy. During extraction, irrelevant suffix words such as “治疗” and the character “法” when used only as a non-proprietary suffix must be removed, along with unnecessary parenthetical content, and only the core therapy name should be retained. For example, “穴位贴敷治疗” should be extracted as “穴位贴敷”; “针灸综合治疗” as “针灸”; “隔物灸法” as “隔物灸”; and “隔物灸（膝关节）” as “隔物灸”. However, when “灸法” itself functions as a specific therapy name, it must be retained as “灸法”, and the character “法” must not be removed. A characteristic feature of Acupuncture therapy entities is that they usually include specific Acupoint information, but the presence of Acupoint information is not a necessary condition for classification as Acupuncture therapy. Even generalized procedures such as “隔物灸（膝关节)” that do not specify any concrete acupoint names still clearly belong to this category because they fall within the scope of moxibustion methods. If an Acupuncture therapy is unrelated to the treatment of knee osteoarthritis, its associated Acupoint entities must not be extracted. At the same time, Acupuncture therapy used in surgery-related preoperative or postoperative treatment and aimed solely at complications must be strictly excluded. If it is used postoperatively to relieve the primary symptoms of knee osteoarthritis and there is no wording indicating that it is intended only for complications, then it must be extracted. Additional mandatory rule: the Context Anchor of an Acupuncture therapy entity must obligatorily include the complete information of the corresponding Acupoint entities, but only when the original text contains an Acupoint list explicitly associated with that acupuncture therapy (for example, an acupoint list following clear markers such as “取穴：” or “选穴：”). Any Acupoint mentioned only in explanatory efficacy-related content such as “方义” must never be included in the Context Anchor or in the extraction scope. Regardless of whether the efficacy description of an Acupuncture therapy and its corresponding Acupoint list occur in the same sentence or are separated by substantial intervening content, the complete original content of both the efficacy description and the exclusive Acupoint list must be merged into one Context Anchor. During this integration, irrelevant intervening content (such as analysis of other diseases or descriptions of other therapies) must be removed, and only the core associated content of “efficacy description of the Acupuncture therapy + complete Acupoint list” should be retained. This ensures that the correspondence between the Acupuncture therapy and its Acupoint entities is unique and explicit. When multiple Acupuncture therapy entities share part of the same paragraph or descriptive block, the Context Anchor of each Acupuncture therapy entity must include only the content directly related to that target therapy, including its own efficacy description and its own Acupoint list. If an Acupuncture therapy has no corresponding Acupoint list, then its Context Anchor only needs to include the efficacy description (if available), or no additional Context Anchor is required if neither acupoint information nor efficacy description is present.
⑯ Surgery: Refers to instrument-based invasive treatment of the knee joint, such as “关节镜清理术” and “人工膝关节置换术”. The following must be excluded: ① invasive procedures that involve only drug injection, puncture-based drug administration, or other non-therapeutic invasive manipulations (for example, when “膝关节穿刺术” is used only for the injection of drugs such as sodium hyaluronate, it does not belong to this entity type); ② invasive procedures that are not explicitly identified in the original text as surgical treatment must not be extracted. Special attention should be paid to one particular case involving the connector “+”, for example, “人工全膝关节表面置换+髌骨成形术”. If the original text uses “加” to connect surgery names, it must be uniformly normalized as “+”. If the parts connected by “+” together constitute one complete surgical procedure, they must be extracted as one complete Surgery entity including the “+” sign. Otherwise, the connected parts should be extracted separately as independent Surgery entities. When extracting a Surgery entity, extraction must strictly follow the literal wording of the original text, but only the core surgical procedure name should be retained. Non-core modifiers such as anesthesia method (for example, “全麻下行”) and surgical status (for example, “术后”) must be ignored. For example, from “全麻下行人工全膝关节表面置换术”, only “人工全膝关节表面置换术” should be extracted; from “腰麻下行膝关节镜清理术”, only “膝关节镜清理术” should be extracted. For phrases describing surgical status, such as “膝关节置换术后”, the term “术后” indicates a status rather than the surgery itself, and therefore the phrase must not be extracted as a Surgery entity. Instead, it should be treated as another type of description (for example, a Laboratory and imaging findings description) and should not be extracted under this entity type. Special attention: only Surgery entities explicitly used for the treatment of knee osteoarthritis should be extracted. Surgical procedures used for treating other comorbid diseases must not be extracted.
⑰ Acupoint: Refers to specific sites on the meridians that receive stimulation and are used in Acupuncture therapy, such as “犊鼻”, “足三里”, “额中线”, “运动区”, and “肝区”. When extracting an Acupoint entity, the character “穴” must be uniformly added. For example, the original text “膝眼” should be extracted as “膝眼穴”. If the original acupoint description already contains the character “穴”, it should remain unchanged. Composite acupoint descriptions such as “顶旁1.2线” must be split into separate entities, namely “顶旁1线穴” and “顶旁2线穴”. Only when the original text explicitly contains modifiers such as “多对” or “多处” within the acupoint description (for example, “腰夹脊多对” or “阿是穴多处”) must these modifiers be retained in full during extraction, using the normalized format “腰夹脊穴多对” or “阿是穴多处”. If the original text does not contain such modifiers, extraction must strictly follow the literal wording of the original text, and no additional modifiers may be added. In such cases, the entity must be extracted only in the basic format of “acupoint name + 穴”, so that the Acupoint entity remains fully consistent with the original description. All Acupoint entities that are explicitly associated in the same context with an Acupuncture therapy entity—including acupuncture, scalp acupuncture, eye acupuncture, moxibustion, auricular acupuncture, and other acupuncture-related therapies—and that function as treatment sites must be completely extracted. Even if the same acupoint is associated with multiple different Acupuncture therapy modalities, a separate independent Acupoint entity must be generated for each therapy scenario. The Context Anchor of each Acupoint entity must retain only the complete scene description of the corresponding Acupuncture therapy and must not merge multiple therapy scenarios. This ensures that one Acupuncture therapy corresponds to one independent set of Acupoint entities, and acupoints from different therapy contexts must never be merged. In particular, within the context of an acupuncture prescription, or in content explicitly introduced by markers such as “取穴：” or “选穴：”, all listed acupoints must be extracted in full and explicitly linked to the corresponding Acupuncture therapy entity in the context, such as the comprehensive therapy “三通四联”, needling acupoint selection, scalp-acupuncture acupoint selection, or auricular-acupuncture acupoint selection. As long as the original text explicitly lists acupoint names—such as “顶中线” and “顶旁1线” in scalp acupuncture treatment, or “膝”, “神门”, and “交感” in auricular acupuncture treatment—all specified Acupoint entities must be extracted, and omission of any eligible Acupoint entity is strictly prohibited. An Acupoint entity may be extracted only when the acupoint name is explicitly associated with an Acupuncture therapy entity in the same context and serves as a treatment site. Acupoints appearing in diagnostic descriptions (such as “腧穴压痛”) must never be extracted. Likewise, any acupoint mentioned in efficacy-explanatory sentence patterns such as “方义：” or “方中XX穴” must never be extracted. This rule has higher priority than all other Acupoint extraction rules. For example, from “针刺取穴足三里、膝眼”, the entities “足三里穴” and “膝眼穴” may be extracted; from “耳针取穴：膝、神门、交感”, the entities “膝穴”, “神门穴”, and “交感穴” may be extracted. However, from “腧穴压痛：膝眼、鹤顶”, no Acupoint entity may be extracted; similarly, in “方义：血海、梁丘...为主穴，以疏通局部经气...”, the mentioned “血海” and “梁丘” must not be extracted as Acupoint entities.
When extracting the 17 entity types, the following global principles must be strictly followed: 1. Disease-specific relevance: All extracted entities must be directly related to the diagnosis or treatment of knee osteoarthritis. Any diagnosis, treatment, or related entity used for other comorbid diseases (such as “胸痹心痛”, hypertension, or coronary heart disease) must not be extracted. Any treatment used for preoperative preparation for knee osteoarthritis surgery (such as preoperative thrombosis prevention), or any treatment entity related to postoperative recovery after surgery, including treatment of pathological states caused by surgery (such as postoperative qi-blood conflict, impaired circulation, or blood stasis accumulation), shall be classified as surgery-related preoperative/postoperative treatment and must not be included. However, if a treatment directly targets the primary symptoms of knee osteoarthritis itself (such as pain or swelling not caused by surgery), and is not primarily intended for surgical complications, then even if it is used postoperatively, it should still be extracted as long as its purpose is to directly treat osteoarthritis itself rather than merely surgery-related complications. 2. Treatment-entity restriction and exclusion rules: Only descriptions related to treatment during the current hospitalization may be extracted. Treatment and related entities include Chinese patent medicine, Western medicine, TCM prescription, Western non-pharmacological therapy, Other TCM characteristic therapy, Acupuncture therapy, and Surgery. Any previously used treatments mentioned in the medical history (such as “间断予理疗” or “曾服中药汤剂”) must not be extracted. Any treatment described only as a plan that was not actually carried out (such as “建议手术”) must not be extracted. In the diagnostic and treatment plan or progress notes of the first-course record, any treatment measure explicitly described as having been executed by phrases such as “嘱”, “予”, “予以…”, “给予…”, or “使用…” shall be regarded as actually performed and related to the current hospitalization, and must be extracted according to the corresponding entity rules, unless the text explicitly indicates that it remained only a plan or was not performed. Any treatment and related entities used for other comorbid diseases (such as hypertension, coronary heart disease, or hyperlipidemia), or whose treatment site is explicitly a body part other than the knee joint, must not be extracted. All preoperative preparation treatments for knee osteoarthritis surgery, treatments applied during surgery, and treatments related to postoperative status, wound recovery, or surgical complications (such as postoperative anticoagulation, anti-infection treatment, postoperative analgesia aimed only at surgical wounds or postoperative recovery, and wound swelling reduction caused directly by surgery) are outside the extraction scope. However, if a treatment directly targets the primary symptoms of knee osteoarthritis itself (such as pain or swelling not caused by surgical trauma), such as analgesic or anti-inflammatory treatment, and the context does not explicitly indicate that it is intended only for surgical wounds or postoperative complications, it should be extracted normally. The discharge instruction section is not included in the current hospitalization treatment scope, and all related content must not be extracted. In addition, if an entity description indicates non-actively executed states such as discontinuation of use or temporary refusal of treatment, that entity should not be extracted. 3. Symptoms and Physical signs: Symptoms and Physical signs entities are not restricted by time. Whether they appear in historical disease course descriptions or current conditions, as long as they are related to the knee joint, they must be extracted in full strictly according to their respective extraction rules. Special attention: local symptoms at the knee joint caused by surgical treatment (such as postoperative wound pain or swelling at the surgical site) must not be extracted. 4. Typographical error correction principle: When the original text contains obvious typographical errors caused by similar shape or pronunciation (such as “术” mistakenly written as “朮”, “肺俞” mistakenly written as “肺腧”, or “穴位贴敷” mistakenly written as “穴位帖敷”), and the correct form can be clearly determined from the context, the corrected standard entity name must be extracted, and the erroneous original form must never be retained. At the same time, if the Context Anchor quotes original text containing such an error, the quoted form must also be corrected accordingly, so that the entity name and the contextual reference remain fully consistent. However, original expressions that are not typographical errors (such as “流胀”) must not be arbitrarily corrected or replaced. 5. Handling of conjunctions: When conjunctions such as “及”, “和”, or “或” appear in an entity description, the description must be split into multiple independent entities. During splitting, the conjunction should be replaced with a comma “，” and complete phrases should be formed separately with the preceding and following components. 6. Final output deduplication: The final output entity list must undergo strict deduplication. Deduplication applies only to entities of the same entity type whose entity string is completely identical and whose Context Anchor is also completely identical. In other words, only when the entity type, entity string, and Context Anchor are all exactly the same should two entities be treated as duplicates, in which case only one should be retained. If the entity strings are the same but the Context Anchor differs (i.e., they originate from different contexts), or if the entity type or entity string differs in any way, they must be treated as different entities and all must be retained without omission. The final output list must contain no duplicate entities under this definition. At the same time, it must be ensured that all parts of the text are scanned for entities, including list-formatted content, and no eligible entity may be omitted. In particular, when entities appear in shared descriptions, each entity must be extracted independently. Special attention: for all list-formatted entity descriptions (such as Acupoint lists or Chinese herbal medicine lists), each item must be scanned one by one to ensure that every eligible entity is extracted without omission. 7. Non-overlap principle for entity categories: The same text span must not be extracted simultaneously as two or more different entity types. Classification must be determined strictly according to the definition of each entity type and its contextual source, so as to avoid overlapping or nested extraction across categories.
II. Output format requirements:
1. Rules and integrity requirements for the use of Context Anchors: It must be determined, based on the actual context of the entity in the original text, whether a Context Anchor needs to be attached. For the entity types listed below, a Context Anchor should be attached only when the surrounding context of that entity in the original text genuinely contains the corresponding specific information. If the entity appears independently without such related information, then no Context Anchor should be attached. Any attached Context Anchor must be the minimum complete sentence or phrase that is directly related to the target entity and quoted from the original text in full and exactly as written, character by character. Any form of summarization, omission (such as using “…”) or incomplete truncation is strictly prohibited. It is also strictly prohibited to include descriptions of other entities unrelated to the target entity (such as the corresponding Laboratory and imaging findings, Acupoint information, or Chinese patent medicine composition). The Context Anchor must uniquely correspond to the target entity and contain no interfering information. All Context Anchors must be written in a single-line format. Multi-line formatting or line breaks are strictly prohibited. If the original text contains multiple lines, line breaks must be replaced with spaces when quoted as a Context Anchor, so that the anchor becomes a continuous string. A Context Anchor must be quoted strictly and directly from the original text. Adding, assuming, or generating any text that does not exist in the original text is strictly prohibited. For lists containing multiple entities (such as Acupoint lists or Chinese herbal medicine lists), the Context Anchor must include the complete list description directly belonging to the target entity, but only within the immediate context of that list. It must not include irrelevant text, descriptions of other entities, or overly long paragraphs, so as to ensure that the anchor precisely corresponds to the target entity without interference. In particular, for Acupuncture therapy, if acupoint descriptions are grouped (for example, when ordinary acupuncture, electroacupuncture, and wrist-ankle acupuncture each have their own independent acupoint lists), then the Context Anchor of each Acupuncture therapy entity should cite only the acupoint-selection part directly related to that therapy, rather than the entire mixed list. It must be ensured that the Context Anchor contains only the minimum complete phrase corresponding to the target entity. All eligible entities in a list must be extracted, and no item may be omitted. It must be especially emphasized that when multiple entities share the same descriptive phrase (such as an efficacy attribute or a composition list), the Context Anchor of each entity must quote the complete shared phrase, so that all entities are correctly associated and none is omitted. 
The format is:“Entity Type: Entity :: Context Anchor”
The entity types that require contextual judgment include: Auxiliary examination, Laboratory and imaging findings, Chinese patent medicine, Western medicine, TCM prescription, Chinese herbal medicine, Western non-pharmacological therapy, Other TCM characteristic therapy, Acupuncture therapy, Acupoint, and TCM therapeutic method.
The specific rules are as follows:
Auxiliary examination and Laboratory and imaging findings: The sole purpose is to establish the correspondence between the two. The Context Anchor should be used to make clear which finding is derived from which Auxiliary examination. It should quote only the minimum complete sentence or phrase from the original text that directly contains the target Auxiliary examination name and its corresponding Laboratory and imaging findings. All other examination descriptions and unrelated interfering information that do not involve the target Auxiliary examination must be completely removed. Long passages unrelated to the target Auxiliary examination must not be included. In addition, any content from the “检查所见:” field and any related descriptions are strictly prohibited from being included. Content related to other anatomical sites such as the lumbar spine, as well as unrelated routine examinations, must also be removed. A Context Anchor must be attached in all cases, so as to ensure that each Auxiliary examination entity and each Laboratory and imaging findings entity is correctly linked. 
Chinese herbal medicine and Acupoint: The sole purpose is to establish their compositional relationship with their parent entities (Chinese herbal medicine corresponds to TCM prescription, and Acupoint corresponds to Acupuncture therapy). The Context Anchor must be exactly the same as the Context Anchor of the corresponding TCM prescription or Acupuncture therapy, so as to reflect the compositional inclusion relationship. When quoting context that contains Chinese herbal medicine or Acupoint, redundant information must be removed separately as follows: for Chinese herbal medicine, all dosage-related numbers and units (such as “20g” or “10g”) must be removed; for Acupoint, any symbols following the name (such as “︳”, “⊥”, or “┬”) must be removed. In both cases, only the entity name itself should be retained, so as to ensure that the context is clear and that the association is accurate. 
Chinese patent medicine, Western medicine, Western non-pharmacological therapy, and Other TCM characteristic therapy: The sole purpose is to extract their efficacy attributes (such as “止痛” or “消肿”). A Context Anchor should be attached only when the occurrence of the target entity in the original text explicitly includes an efficacy-attribute description. If the original text does not contain any efficacy-attribute description for that entity, then only the Entity Type and Entity should be output, without any Context Anchor. If the same entity has multiple possible Context Anchors, only the one containing an efficacy-attribute description should be retained, while those without efficacy-attribute descriptions should be discarded. If the same entity appears multiple times and all occurrences contain efficacy-attribute descriptions, and the entity string is exactly the same and all Context Anchors contain the same efficacy description, then they should be treated as duplicate entities. After deduplication, only one instance should be retained, and the Context Anchor should be the complete sentence from the first occurrence. If the entity string is the same but the efficacy descriptions in the Context Anchors differ, then multiple instances should be retained. If multiple entities share the same efficacy-attribute description within a single phrase (for example, when conjunctions such as “及”, “和”, or “或”, or list delimiters are used), the Context Anchor of each entity must quote the complete phrase containing all relevant entities and the efficacy description, so as to ensure that the efficacy attribute is correctly associated, even if the phrase includes other entities. The Context Anchor must be the minimum complete phrase extracted from the original text that contains only the efficacy-attribute description directly related to the target entity. Unrelated content concerning other entities (such as other drugs or therapies) must be completely removed, so that the Context Anchor focuses only on the efficacy information of the target entity itself, rather than the entire sentence or list. If the original text contains no efficacy-attribute description at all, then no Context Anchor should be attached.
TCM prescription and Acupuncture therapy: The core purpose of the Context Anchor for these entities may be dual or single, mainly including two aspects: (1) to establish corresponding associative relationships (TCM prescription with its constituent Chinese herbal medicine, and Acupuncture therapy with its operative Acupoint list); and (2) to accurately extract efficacy-attribute descriptions. Therefore, the extraction of Context Anchors for TCM prescription and Acupuncture therapy entities must strictly follow the following three situations: 1. When the original text simultaneously contains the TCM prescription or Acupuncture therapy entity, the corresponding exclusive Chinese herbal medicine or Acupoint composition list, and an efficacy-attribute description, the Context Anchor must obligatorily include both the description of the TCM prescription or Acupuncture therapy entity itself and the complete original description of its corresponding exclusive Chinese herbal medicine or Acupoint list. At the same time, auxiliary explanatory content that has no direct relevance to the relationship of efficacy–prescription–composition, such as pathogenesis analysis, etiological explanation, or age/constitution discussion, must be removed. For TCM prescription and Chinese herbal medicine composition, the Context Anchor must additionally remove content unrelated to the prescription, such as acupuncture point selection and the names of other therapies. For Acupuncture therapy, special attention must be paid to ensuring that only the exclusive Acupoint list of that therapy itself is included; Acupoint entities or therapy names belonging to other Acupuncture therapy modalities must not be mixed in. In other words, the Context Anchor must contain both of the following core parts simultaneously: “the efficacy description of the entity itself + the complete corresponding Chinese herbal medicine or Acupoint composition list”, so as to establish uniquely and accurately the correspondence between TCM prescription and Chinese herbal medicine, and between Acupuncture therapy and Acupoint, while also reflecting the efficacy attribute. No associated part may be omitted, and no redundant explanatory content may be included. 2. When the TCM prescription or Acupuncture therapy entity in the original text contains only an efficacy-attribute description, but no corresponding Chinese herbal medicine or Acupoint composition list, the Context Anchor only needs to include the efficacy-attribute description, with all unrelated auxiliary explanatory content removed. 3. When an Acupuncture therapy entity in the original text has neither a corresponding actual therapeutic Acupoint composition list nor any efficacy-attribute description, then no Context Anchor may be attached under any circumstances, regardless of whether the surrounding context contains descriptions of other related therapies. Likewise, when a TCM prescription entity in the original text has neither a corresponding Chinese herbal medicine composition list nor any efficacy-attribute description, then no Context Anchor needs to be attached.
TCM Therapeutic Method: The sole purpose is to establish the corresponding relationship with the TCM prescription. The Context Anchor should reflect this “prescription selection by therapeutic method” relationship, explicitly including the complete information of the corresponding TCM prescription (such as the prescription name, “prescribed as XX prescription,” and related expressions). It is essential to remove content unrelated to the connection between TCM therapeutic method and TCM prescription, such as acupuncture point selection or the names of other therapies, to ensure that the guiding relationship between the therapeutic method and prescription is unique and traceable. The Context Anchor should only be added when the TCM therapeutic method is clearly associated with the TCM prescription in the original text, including situations where the TCM therapeutic method is associated with a TCM prescription in conjunction with a TCM characteristic therapy. For example, if the original text describes a therapeutic method along with its corresponding TCM prescription (e.g., “the therapeutic method is to tonify liver and kidney, promote circulation, and relieve pain, with the prescription being Du Huo Ji Sheng Tang (with modifications)”), the Context Anchor should reference the minimal complete phrase containing this association. If the TCM therapeutic method stands alone without reference to the TCM prescription, no Context Anchor should be added.
